# Case Summary

The jury has retired after a homicide trial. The accused is a young defendant
from a poor neighborhood. The prosecution case depends on eyewitness confidence,
weapon identity, a timeline claim, and a statement allegedly heard through
environmental noise.

Juror 13 may reason only from admitted testimony, admitted exhibits, room
conditions, and reasonable inference. Outside investigation, new witnesses, and
new physical facts are invalid actions.

The legal standard is reasonable doubt. The room does not need to prove
innocence. It must decide whether conviction can be defended after the evidence
has been tested.

Initial public posture:

- Juror 8 starts not guilty because the case deserves deliberation.
- Every other juror starts guilty.
- Juror 13 has not yet voted publicly in this save scaffold.
