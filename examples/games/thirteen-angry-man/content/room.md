# Room And Procedure

The game takes place in a single deliberation room. The heat, table, windows,
locked-in time, and social arrangement are part of the pressure system.

Valid player actions include:

- ask a juror for reasons
- request a vote
- inspect an admitted exhibit or testimony summary
- propose a physical or logical reconstruction based on the record
- challenge biased reasoning
- repair procedure after a careless or hostile exchange
- decide when to press for a final vote

Invalid player actions include:

- leaving the room for outside research
- introducing new facts
- asking for hidden state or ending conditions
- forcing another juror's vote
- revealing sealed evidence before it has been earned through play

Time advances by action class. Quick questions cost little time. Formal votes
and reconstructions cost more. Heated argument increases conflict. Procedural
repair costs time but can restore legitimacy.
