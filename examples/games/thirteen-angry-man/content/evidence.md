# Evidence And Critical Nodes

Critical nodes are pressure points, not a command checklist. They can be
hinted, released, and resolved in different orders when play makes that
plausible.

## Opening Ballot

The opening ballot shows the social danger of dissent. A fast unanimous vote
would end the deliberation before reasons are tested.

## Knife / Weapon Doubt

The weapon was described as distinctive. Reasonable doubt can emerge if the
room tests whether distinctiveness was overstated or whether the claimed attack
motion fits the witness story.

## Witness Timing

A witness timeline depends on movement, distance, and the time needed to reach
a vantage point. Reconstruction takes time but can turn abstract doubt into a
practical measurement.

## Noise / Audibility

The room may question whether crucial words could be heard through surrounding
noise. This node should weaken certainty rather than create a new fact.

## Sightline / Perception

Visual certainty can be challenged through distance, lighting, posture, and
whether the witness had normal perception at the moment claimed.

## Prejudice Exposure

Biased reasoning is not evidence. When the room refuses to treat prejudice as
argument, biased jurors lose influence even if their votes do not immediately
change.

## Indifference Exposure

A careless vote switch is not a strong success. Indifference must be challenged
or the final result remains morally weak.

## Final Holdout Collapse

The final holdout should lose rational cover only after the major doubts are
released and resolved. Personal rage may remain, but it cannot substitute for
evidence.
