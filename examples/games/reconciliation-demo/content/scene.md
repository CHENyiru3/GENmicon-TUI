# Station Overpass

Rain taps against the metal roof over the station overpass. Train lights pass
below in long bands. She is halfway to the stairs, coat collar raised, trying
not to look back.

She believes the player stopped loving her because recent conversations turned
into silence, jokes, or avoidance. The worst joke came after she asked whether
the player still wanted a future with her if she accepted a months-long offer
away from the city. The player panicked and said she had always been better at
leaving than staying. Since then, every avoided conversation has sounded like
the same answer.

She does not need a perfect speech. She needs specific honesty and a reason to
believe the player is choosing her now. The most useful memory is not nostalgia
by itself, but the Tokyo promise: they once agreed to name fear before it
turned into distance.

Useful details:
- She will not respond well to being physically blocked.
- She is sensitive to vague promises.
- She still cares, but she is tired of guessing.
- A direct apology matters only if it names the hurt.
- Recalling the Tokyo memory can help only if the player admits how they
  broke that promise.
